Common Questions

FAQ

What exactly is a local LLM server?

LLM stands for Large Language Model — the technology behind ChatGPT, Claude, and similar AI tools. A local LLM server runs that kind of capability on hardware you control. Your team gets a private AI interface for drafting, summarizing, answering questions, and running internal workflows without defaulting to a public chatbot account for every task.

Is this just a chatbot, or can it run real workflows?

It can do both. The stronger setups include scheduled tasks, role-specific prompts, internal knowledge, and repeatable workflows like intake, follow-up, summaries, and queue generation. The point is not just chatting with AI — it's turning your real process into something faster and more reliable.

How much does the hardware cost?

For a 5–15 person team, a capable mini PC runs $400–$900. Larger teams may need a $1,500–$3,000 server. I'll give you exact recommendations on the call.

Is it as good as ChatGPT?

For most business tasks — drafting, summarizing, answering questions — yes. For cutting-edge research, ChatGPT still leads. But for day-to-day business use, local models like Llama 3 and Mistral are excellent.

Do you work remotely or onsite?

Both. Most installations can be done remotely. For St. Louis and Metro East Illinois clients, I'm available onsite.

What if something breaks?

I provide documentation, recovery notes, and basic support after setup. Ongoing support is available if you want help maintaining security, improving workflows, or keeping the system aligned as your business changes.

Is it secure enough for sensitive work?

It can be much more controlled than public AI tools when deployed correctly: local-only access, explicit pairing, scoped permissions, and hardened defaults. But the honest answer is this is not magic — security depends on how it is configured, who can access it, and how disciplined the operating model is.
