Common Questions

FAQ

What exactly is a local LLM server?

LLM stands for Large Language Model — the technology behind ChatGPT, Claude, and similar AI tools. A local LLM server runs that same technology on hardware inside your building. Your team gets a private AI chat interface accessible from any device on your network. No internet connection required to use it, no data sent to third-party AI companies, and no subscription fees after setup.

How much does the hardware cost?

For a 5–15 person team, a capable mini PC runs $400–$900. Larger teams may need a $1,500–$3,000 server. I'll give you exact recommendations on the call.

Is it as good as ChatGPT?

For most business tasks — drafting, summarizing, answering questions — yes. For cutting-edge research, ChatGPT still leads. But for day-to-day business use, local models like Llama 3 and Mistral are excellent.

Do you work remotely or onsite?

Both. Most installations can be done remotely. For St. Louis and Metro East Illinois clients, I'm available onsite.

What if something breaks?

I provide documentation and basic support after setup. Ongoing support contracts are available — we can discuss options on the call.
