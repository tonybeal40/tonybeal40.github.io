CEO Screening Questions

Prepared for Ian Lenz  ·  Confidential

Question 1
Can you share specific examples from your time at ProForm or Natoli where you worked directly or closely with die casting, sand casting, investment casting, CNC machining, or sheet metal fabrication? This could include hands-on work or coordination with suppliers or NPI processes.
At ProForm, my role was on the commercial and coordination side. I worked directly with customers and internal teams on projects involving CNC machining and fabricated components — aligning requirements with shop capabilities, supporting quoting, and moving NPI through early stages including supplier coordination and first article submissions. Accounts included companies like Forest Concepts and Northrop Grumman. At Natoli, I managed technical account relationships where customer requirements had to be translated into production action. That meant coordinating between procurement and engineering contacts at companies like Duracell, Pfizer, Eli Lilly, Hims & Hers, and BASF — making sure both sides stayed aligned and moving.
Question 2
Can you describe your experience working with overseas or Asian suppliers? How have you handled things like evening calls, corrective actions, or coordinating across time zones?
I didn't directly own overseas supplier relationships, but I coordinated across stakeholders and time constraints regularly. I'm comfortable with off-hours communication and keeping corrective actions moving when you can't just walk to someone's desk. Staying organized, communicating clearly in writing, and not letting things stall while waiting on a time zone gap — that's the work, and I'm comfortable operating that way.
Question 3
Can you provide examples of times you identified upsell opportunities or expanded existing accounts? How have you managed technical conversations with customers in those situations?
At both ProForm and Natoli I grew accounts by staying close to the customer and asking the right questions. At ProForm, a big part of how the program scaled was identifying adjacent needs within existing accounts and positioning additional capabilities before the customer went looking elsewhere. I approach technical conversations by listening first, clarifying requirements, then aligning those needs with what we can actually deliver — not overselling and not underselling.
Question 4
Can you tell me about your experience working in a very small, high-growth, or nimble environment? What was your role on the team, and how did you adapt to wearing multiple hats?
Most of my career has been in small, fast-moving environments. At ProForm I built the program infrastructure from scratch — tracking systems, quality documentation, account management — while also owning customer relationships and driving new business. There was no playbook. I figured it out as we went and kept execution moving. That environment is actually where I operate best: less structure, more ownership, results over process.
