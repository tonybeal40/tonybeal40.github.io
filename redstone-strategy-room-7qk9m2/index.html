01 · Company Brief

What Redstone really sells

Redstone appears positioned as an American-owned manufacturing partner helping U.S. companies reduce global production risk through sourcing discipline, supplier control, and execution reliability.

Core model

Casting, CNC machining, sheet metal, fabrication, and global sourcing coordinated around delivery confidence.

Strategic angle

Expansion beyond China into Mexico, India, and Taiwan aligns with tariff pressure and supply-chain risk diversification.

Leadership context

Eric Millinger is publicly identifiable as President, with background tied to international supply chain and U.S. precision capability expansion.

02 · StoryBrand View

Customer story, pressure, and outcome

Customer

OEM, sourcing leader, operations leader, or engineering buyer carrying launch and supplier accountability.

External Problem

Cost, quality, tariff, lead-time, and supplier inconsistency across global manufacturing.

Internal Problem

They do not want surprises, failed launches, or awkward customer explanations.

Philosophical Problem

Buyers should not get punished for outsourcing globally when disciplined execution is possible.

Guide

Redstone as the execution guide that sources, validates, launches, manages, and solves.

Plan

Source → validate → launch → manage → troubleshoot → expand trust.

Success

Delivered parts, lower risk, cleaner communication, and repeatable customer trust.

Failure

Late shipments, poor quality, cost surprises, strained relationships, and damaged credibility.

04 · Troubleshooting Proof

Evidence from pressure situations

Natoli

Challenge: High-pressure execution environment with handoff friction and accountability stress.
Action: Tightened communication rhythm, clarified ownership, and pushed issues toward closure instead of drift.
Why it matters: Redstone needs someone who reduces surprises before they become customer pain.

Proform

Challenge: Misalignment risk between moving parts, expectations, and execution follow-through.
Action: Drove clearer plans, aligned responsibilities, and tightened issue visibility.
Why it matters: Supplier development is often less about theory and more about disciplined coordination.

Wicks

Challenge: Inconsistent follow-through and fragmented ownership.
Action: Created tighter tracking and closure expectations around what mattered most.
Why it matters: Redstone wins when execution loops stay tight, visible, and calm under pressure.
05 · 30-60-90 Plan

Month one diagnosis. Month two control. Month three repeatability.

0-30

Learn the real system

  • Map customers, suppliers, and internal handoffs.
  • Identify common failure points and communication gaps.
  • Learn what creates escalations, misses, and avoidable surprises.
31-60

Tighten execution rhythm

  • Improve visibility around launches, issues, and status ownership.
  • Clarify escalation paths and communication discipline.
  • Reduce surprises through better follow-up and issue tracking.
61-90

Stabilize and expand

  • Surface process improvements with practical leverage.
  • Build a more repeatable customer-facing execution model.
  • Identify where cleaner execution supports account growth.
06 · Tier 1 / 2 / 3 Targets

Show judgment, not random logo collecting

Tier 1

High-complexity, high-value OEMs where execution failures are expensive and supplier reliability is critical.

Tier 2

Growth manufacturers under tariff, supplier, quality, or lead-time pressure that need a stronger operating partner.

Tier 3

Mid-market opportunities that need flexible sourcing across U.S. / Mexico / Asia without enterprise overhead.
