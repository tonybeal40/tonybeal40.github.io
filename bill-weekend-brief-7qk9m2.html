Executive Summary

This is not a marketing agency.

This is a visibility and growth system for blue-collar and owner-led businesses. The offer is simple: we help owners see where money is going, build a brand people trust, and create a cleaner path from attention to signed jobs.

Core positioning

We take what is in the owner’s head, turn it into a working business system, and keep building around that vision with AI, content, data, and execution.

Problem and Opportunity

Successful owners are still flying blind.

The typical contractor or trade owner can sell, can work, and can survive. What they usually cannot do is see their business clearly enough to scale it without stress, wasted margin, or total dependence on themselves.

Where money leaks

Materials, labor drift, downtime, underbidding, and scattered purchases are invisible until after the damage is done.

Where growth stalls

Word of mouth works until the owner wants consistency, better jobs, or a business that does not depend on one person being everywhere.

Why the market is open

Most small operators are underserved. They do not need enterprise software. They need a guide who can make complexity disappear.

30 / 60 / 90 Model

The product is a transformation, not a tool.

Days 0-30

Clarity and foundation

Stop guessing. Capture how the business actually works and build the first operating layer around the owner’s reality.

  • Interview the owner and extract the true pain, constraints, and goals.
  • Set up the voice-note workflow so ideas, updates, and job data can be spoken instead of typed.
  • Build or refine the website around StoryBrand messaging and trust.
  • Create the first visibility dashboard for jobs, labor, costs, and estimate drift.
Days 31-60

Positioning and authority

Make the company look bigger, clearer, and more credible than competitors who still rely on a weak online presence.

  • Turn voice notes into posts, articles, updates, and proof-driven brand content.
  • Refine the company message using StoryBrand: customer is the hero, business is the guide.
  • Strengthen Google presence, reviews, visual proof, and local trust signals.
  • Build a repeatable content rhythm without asking the owner to become a marketer.
Days 61-90

Lead flow and optimization

Turn visibility into revenue. Refine bidding, tighten follow-up, and build the first predictable growth system.

  • Install lead capture and basic pipeline visibility.
  • Track lead to estimate to close so sales conversations improve over time.
  • Use actual job data to improve estimates, margin targets, and scheduling assumptions.
  • Create a growth loop the owner can feel instead of another disconnected marketing expense.
Target Market

Sell to businesses with money, chaos, and a real owner.

The first market should stay tight. The best fit is owner-led businesses in construction, trades, small manufacturing, and service businesses that are old-school, competent, and under-digitized.

Ideal firm profile

  • 2-15 employees
  • $300K-$3M annual revenue
  • Owner still involved in sales or operations
  • Strong reputation, weak systems

Best entry verticals

  • General contractors and remodelers
  • Roofing, HVAC, concrete, electrical, plumbing
  • Home services with field crews
  • Small fabrication or manufacturing shops

What they already spend

  • Angi or HomeAdvisor: often $500-$3,000 monthly
  • Marketing agencies: often $1,000-$5,000 monthly
  • Websites: usually one-time cost with little operating value
  • The gap: almost nobody gives them visibility plus growth

Operating Split

Bill sells trust. Tony builds the machine.

The structure only works if roles stay simple. Bill should not become the tech guy. Tony should not become the guy chasing every cold call. The model wins because each person stays in strength.

Bill · CEO / Sales

Front-of-house credibility

  • Has 23+ years of real owner experience.
  • Can talk to contractors without sounding corporate.
  • Can frame pain around waste, stress, margin, and growth.
  • Closes with trust, not jargon.
What Bill does daily
  • Make 10-20 calls, texts, or reach-outs to owners and referrals.
  • Open with simple pain questions instead of pitching features.
  • Bring qualified interest into a strategy conversation.
  • Stay focused on conversations and closing.
Tony · Systems / Delivery

  • Packages the StoryBrand narrative into a brand that feels real.
  • Uses AI to build faster without making the work feel generic.
What Tony does daily

Offer and Pricing

Price the outcome, not the software.

Recommended Model

Simple 90-day growth retainer

$2,000/month $2,000-$3,000 setup + $1,500-$2,500/month

The simplest story to sell is a flat monthly fee with a 90-day minimum. It keeps the conversation centered on outcomes and avoids over-explaining how the system is assembled.

  • Split target = $10,000/month each
  • Best capacity range = 8-12 clients
  • Hard cap = 15 high-touch accounts
The offer becomes stronger when attached to one or two local proof stories.

  • Visibility dashboard and weekly decision report.

  • Content repurposing from field updates, ideas, and wins.
  • Lead capture, follow-up view, and credibility assets.
  • A consultant relationship that feels hands-on, not outsourced.

Realistic Build

What it takes to make this real.

This can start lean. The main investment is time, trust, and consistency. The first version does not need a giant software budget to work.

Cost and timeline
  • Startup tool cost can stay under roughly $300/month at the beginning.
  • Week 1: finalize offer, naming direction, and demo pages.
  • Week 2: Bill starts conversations and books first meetings.
  • Weeks 3-4: close first client or refine offer based on live objections.
  • Months 2-3: build to 3-5 clients, tighten delivery, and gather proof.
What could kill it
  • Trying to sell AI instead of business outcomes.
  • Going after owners who are broke, skeptical, or not ready to change.
  • Overbuilding before the first paying client.
  • Not documenting wins, margin improvement, or saved time.
  • Letting the offer turn into custom chaos for every client.
